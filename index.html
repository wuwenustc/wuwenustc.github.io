Short Bio

Currently, I am an Associate Researcher at the Peng Cheng Laboratory, Shenzhen. I worked as a Post-doctoral fellow with the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Waterloo. I received the Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering from University of Waterloo, Waterloo, ON, Canada, in 2019. My Ph.D. advisor was Prof. Xuemin Shen, and my thesis is on Design and Analysis of Beamforming in mmWave Networks. I received the B.S. degree in Information Engineering from South China University of Technology, Guangzhou, China, and the M.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from University of Science and Technology of China, Hefei, China, in 2012 and 2015, respectively. My research interests include millimeter-wave networks, RAN slicing, edge intelligence, and AI-empowered wireless networking.
