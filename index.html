Short Bio

Currently, I am an Associate Researcher at the Peng Cheng Laboratory, Shenzhen, Guangdong, China. From 2019 to 2021, I worked as a Post-doctoral fellow with the Broadband Communications Research (BBCR) Lab, Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Waterloo. I received the Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering from University of Waterloo, Waterloo, ON, Canada, in 2019. My Ph.D. advisor was Prof. Xuemin Shen, and my thesis is on Design and Analysis of Beamforming in mmWave Networks. I received the B.S. degree in Information Engineering from South China University of Technology, Guangzhou, China, and the M.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from University of Science and Technology of China, Hefei, China, in 2012 and 2015, respectively. My research interests include digital twin, network slicing, network intelligence, and millimeter-wave networks.

I am looking for highly motivated visiting PhD students and Postdoc Fellows to join my group. Feel free to send your CV to me via email.
