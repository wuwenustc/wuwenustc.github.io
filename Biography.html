Biography

Dr. Wen Wu is an associate researcher at the Frontier Research Center at Peng Cheng Laboratory, Shenzhen, Guangdong, China. He received the B.E. degree in Information Engineering from South China University of Technology, Guangzhou, China, and the M.E. degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Science and Technology of China, Hefei, China, in 2012 and 2015, respectively. He received his Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Waterloo, Waterloo, ON, Canada, in 2019, and then worked as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Waterloo, before joining the Peng Cheng Laboratory. Dr. Wu’s research focuses on 6G networks, network virtualization, network intelligence, and networking for AI. Dr. Wu is a Senior Member of IEEE and China Institute of Communications (CIC). He served as the Distinguished Researcher (Level-C) in the Peng Cheng Laboratory, and received the Award for Excellence (Early Career Researcher) from IEEE Hyper-Intelligence Technical Committee and the Best Paper Award at IEEE/CIC ICCC 2022.

Dr. Wu serves/served on TPCs and organizing committees for a number of ACM/IEEE conferences, including the Workshop TPC Co-Chair for IEEE INFOCOM and ICCC, Symposium Chair of IEEE Future Networks World Forum, Track Chair of EAI CollaborateCom, and Workshop Co-Chair for IEEE IPCCC and IEEE HPCC. Dr. Wu serves/served on the editorial board for Hindawi WCMC, China Communications, Frontiers in IoT, and Springer PPNA. Dr. Wu serves/served as a Technical Program Committee (TPC) Member for several international conferences, including the IEEE ICC, Globecom, PIMRC, VTC, Mobicom Workshop, ICCC, and WCSP.
